**Today's headline:** Bud goes native inside Banno via Jack Henry — ASA's primary distribution channel now ships a competing transaction-intelligence layer by default.

## TL;DR

1. Jack Henry launched transaction enrichment powered by Bud Financial directly inside Banno, putting a Compass/One View competitor native to ASA's largest channel [REF 1][REF 2]. 
2. CFPB designated FDX as the first official Section 1033 standard-setting body, while separately notifying the court that DOJ/OLC has determined CFPB cannot lawfully draw funds from the Federal Reserve — fixing the technical baseline while gutting the enforcement engine [REF 3][REF 4]. 
3. Akoya is publicly lobbying CFPB to ban screen scraping and narrow secondary data use; combined with Wells Fargo and PNC reportedly pressuring fintechs onto Akoya's rails, a bank-controlled aggregation pathway is hardening [REF 5][REF 6].

## By roadmap area

**Vault.** Akoya's lobbying push to eliminate screen scraping and JPMorgan's renegotiated paid-data pact with Yodlee both point to an aggregation layer where bank-permissioned, fee-bearing API access becomes the default and screen-scraped paths get squeezed out [REF 5][REF 6][REF 7]. ASA Vault's permissioned-by-design posture is on the right side of this, but the cost structure shifts if data-access tolls spread. Recommendation: Investigate JPMorgan-Yodlee fee terms and model Vault unit economics under a paid-API regime; **Decision needed by 2026-05-16** on whether to publish a pricing-transparency stance.

**Compass.** No material signal today.

**Auth.** No material signal today.

**Verify.** No material signal today.

**Pay.** No material signal today.

**One View.** Jack Henry's Bud-powered transaction enrichment ships natively inside Banno, the channel ASA depends on for One View distribution [REF 1][REF 2]. This is a default-on competitor at the FI level, not a third-party app. Recommendation: **Decision needed by 2026-05-09** on whether BD pursues a counter-positioning conversation with top 20 Banno FIs before the Bud rollout reaches them.

**Forecast.** Same Banno/Bud integration affects Forecast's enrichment dependency inside Jack Henry FIs [REF 1][REF 2]. Forecast's differentiation now needs to lean on prediction quality rather than enrichment depth, since enrichment is becoming a Banno-native commodity. Recommendation: Investigate whether Forecast can ingest Bud-enriched transactions as input rather than competing on enrichment.

**Horizontal.** The 1033 picture split today: FDX is now the formally recognized standard-setting body, but CFPB told a federal court it cannot lawfully draw Federal Reserve funds per DOJ/OLC [REF 3][REF 4]. Compliance scaffolding is set; enforcement is not. Recommendation: Monitor the NTEU v. Vought docket and FDX technical specs; treat FDX conformance as the binding constraint regardless of CFPB enforcement capacity.
